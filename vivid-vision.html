Vivid Vision: A Remarkable Tool For Aligning Your Business Around a Shared Vision of the Future: by Cameron Herold

PART ONE: WHY YOU NEED A VIVID VISION

INTRODUCTION: THE CURRENT BUSINESS LANDSCAPE

  • Core Principle: To align a team – ensuring everyone moves in the same direction at the same speed – you must develop a detailed vision of the future and share it effectively.

CHAPTER 1: THE POWER OF A SHARED VISION

  • Implied but not stated: A shared vision is critical for organizational success.

CHAPTER 2: THE SOLUTION: VIVID VISION

  • Leadership Responsibility: As the leader, if you fail to provide your team with clear insights into your vision for the company, you are hindering their ability to contribute to its realization. You are the bottleneck, not them.
  • Decision-Making Framework: A clear vision enables effective decision-making. Knowing your destination allows you to make and apply decisions that move you toward it.
  • Clarity and Specificity: A Vivid Vision must be clear, specific, and free of ambiguity.

CHAPTER 3: BEYOND THE "BHAG" (Big Hairy Audacious Goal)

  • Avoid Incrementalism: Many companies base their plans solely on their current state. A Vivid Vision requires a more ambitious approach.
  • Focus on "What," Not "How": When crafting your Vivid Vision, focus on describing the desired future state (the "what"). Don't worry about the specific steps to get there (the "how") at this stage.
  • CEO's Role: The CEO's primary responsibility is to define where the organization is going.
  • Leadership Team's Role: The leadership team's role is to figure out how to achieve the Vivid Vision.
  • The Three-Year Timeframe:
    • A three-year timeframe encourages innovation.
    • It allows for proactive project scheduling, rather than being driven by immediate necessity.

PART TWO: HOW TO WRITE A VIVID VISION

CHAPTER 4: PREPARING TO WRITE

  • Find Inspiration: Start by finding an inspiring environment that frees your mind.
  • Communicator's Mindset: Your job is to communicate your vision clearly and effectively.
  • Personal and Authentic: The Vivid Vision is a personal reflection of your aspirations for the company. It's your duty to articulate and share that vision, then build a team capable of achieving it.
  • Alignment and Pruning: Hire individuals who align with your vision and remove those who obstruct its realization.

CHAPTER 5: A DIFFERENT KIND OF EXERCISE: THE MIND MAP

  • Mind Mapping: Use a Mind Map to visually organize your thoughts. Start with the company's core in the center and branch out to different areas.
  • Business Application:
    • Place yourself (or the company) at the center.
    • Branch out to key areas of the business (e.g., marketing, sales, engineering, operations, finance).
    • Describe each branch with 3-4 concise bullet points.
    • Consider every aspect: culture, atmosphere, informal codes of conduct, financial environment, physical setting, etc.
    • Time Travel Exercise: Imagine stepping out of a time machine three years from now and walking through your company. What do you observe? What is typical of the business in this future state?
  • Mind Map as an Outline: The Mind Map serves as the initial outline for your Vivid Vision.
    • Brain Dump: The goal is to capture all your thoughts, no matter how unconventional they may seem.
    • Exhaustive Detail: Describe each department, the company culture, what employees and the media say about you – every aspect of the business, until you've captured everything.
  • CEO Ownership: While input from others is valuable, the CEO must be the primary author of the Vivid Vision.
  • Professional Polish: While the initial vision originates from the CEO, consider engaging a skilled writer to refine and polish the language.
  • Visual Appeal: Present your Vivid Vision in a visually engaging way, using graphic design or other elements to inspire the reader without being distracting.
  • Document Length: Aim for a final document of up to four pages in length.

CHAPTER 6: LEARNING FROM EXAMPLES: MY FIRST VIVID VISION

  • Sharing is Key: The most effective way to turn dreams into reality is to share your vision with others.
  • Mental Image: The Vivid Vision is a vivid, detailed mental image of what the company will look, feel, and act like three years in the future.

PART THREE: HOW TO ROLL IT OUT

CHAPTER 7: THE INTERNAL ROLLOUT: INSPIRING YOUR TEAM

  • Creating Awe and Wonder: When your team reads your Vivid Vision, it should evoke a sense of awe, excitement, and inspiration.
  • Embrace Discomfort: Step outside your comfort zone to create a truly compelling vision.
  • Internal Rollout Process:
    1. Hard Copy Distribution: Provide each team member with a printed copy of the Vivid Vision.
    2. Read Aloud: Read the Vivid Vision aloud to the team.
    3. Gauge Reactions: As the CEO, observe the team's reactions carefully. Be prepared to address any lack of engagement or alignment.
    4. Highlight Inspiration: Have each employee circle or underline the sentences or phrases that most excite and inspire them.
    5. Focus on Understanding: The initial rollout is not a time for debate or discussion. It's an opportunity for everyone to understand the CEO's vision.
  • Quarterly Review: Revisit the Vivid Vision every quarter.
    1. Highlight Accomplishments: Highlight in green the parts that have been achieved in the last quarter.
    2. Highlight Progress: Highlight in yellow the parts that are currently in progress.
    3. Prioritize Next Steps: Review each sentence and determine the projects that need to be tackled next, in a logical sequence.
    4. Quarterly Planning: Use the Vivid Vision as a roadmap for planning the next quarter's activities.
    5. Maintain Alignment: This quarterly exercise keeps the entire organization aligned and focused on the shared vision.
  • Attraction and Repulsion: A bold vision will attract individuals who are excited about it and repel those who are not.
  • Embrace Attrition: It's acceptable to lose people who don't align with the Vivid Vision.
  • Immutable Vision: Treat the Vivid Vision as a foundational document, like the Ten Commandments – set in stone.

CHAPTER 8: THE EXTERNAL ROLLOUT: SHARING WITH THE WORLD

  • Introduction to Stakeholders: When presenting the Vivid Vision to external parties, explain, "This is what our company will look like in the near future. We know it doesn't look like this today, but this is where we will be in three years."
  • Simultaneous Release: Share the Vivid Vision broadly and simultaneously through various channels (e.g., email, website, social media).
  • Consistent Reinforcement: Regularly share the Vivid Vision to remind stakeholders of what you're building.
  • External Contributions: Recognize that external parties (customers, suppliers, partners) can play a role in achieving the vision.
  • Future Focus: Emphasize the future state depicted in the Vivid Vision.
  • Roadmap Analogy: The Vivid Vision serves as a roadmap to the future.
  • Timing: Wait one quarter after the internal rollout before sharing the Vivid Vision externally.
  • Recruitment Tool: Share the Vivid Vision with potential employees to assess their fit with the company's direction.
  • Supplier Alignment: The Vivid Vision helps suppliers understand your long-term goals.
  • Glimpse into the Future: The primary purpose of sharing the Vivid Vision externally is to give stakeholders a glimpse into the company's future.
  • Delegation of "How": Remember, your role as CEO is to define the "what," not the "how." Delegate the execution to your team.
  • Persistence: Getting others to see your vision requires consistent and persistent communication.

PART FOUR: MAKE IT COME TRUE

CHAPTER 9: REVERSE ENGINEERING: FROM VISION TO ACTION

  • Idea Capture: Team members may have ideas inspired by the Vivid Vision. Capture these ideas, even if they don't directly fit into the current plan. Consider creating an "inspiration board" or a dedicated space for these ideas.
  • Building Belief: Collecting and acknowledging these ideas can help foster belief in the Vivid Vision and boost morale.

CHAPTER 10: MAINTAINING THE VISION: CONSISTENCY IS KEY

  • Regular Review: Revisit the Vivid Vision regularly – as an organization, every quarter.
  • Meeting Integration:
    • Read at least one paragraph of the Vivid Vision in every meeting with three or more people.
    • Begin every strategic planning meeting by reviewing the document.
  • Team Alignment: The Vivid Vision's purpose is to create and maintain alignment within the team.
  • CEO's Role: As CEO, your job is to align, inspire, and then empower your team to execute.

CHAPTER 11: SUBSEQUENT VIVID VISIONS: EVOLUTION OVER TIME

  • Implied but not stated: Creating subsequent Vivid Visions is about the evolution of the company over time.

CHAPTER 12: BENEFITS BIG AND SMALL: THE IMPACT OF A VIVID VISION

  • Customer Relationships: Customers are not just buying products or services; they are entering into a relationship with your company.
  • Importance of Branding: A strong brand and reputation are essential for building and maintaining relationships.
  • The "Wow" Factor: Creating a "wow" factor for your brand is invaluable.

CHAPTER 13: YOUR PERSONAL JOURNEY: APPLYING VIVID VISION TO YOUR LIFE

  • Personal Vision: Apply the principles of Vivid Vision to your personal life.
  • Key Areas: Project yourself three years into the future and describe in detail what you see in these areas:
    • Fitness:
      • How does your appearance differ from today?
      • What is your physical condition?
      • How healthy are you?
    • Faith:
      • What does your spiritual life look like in three years?
    • Finance:
      • What progress have you made in your financial situation?
      • Be specific; include concrete figures.
    • Family:
      • What is your relationship with your partner like?
      • How have you grown closer?
      • Do you share more interests?
      • Are you a better life partner in three years?
      • Consider your relationships with parents and other family members.
    • Friends:
      • Are you the kind of friend you would want to have?

CHAPTER 14: A VISION FOR YOUR FAMILY: EXTENDING THE PRINCIPLES

  • Family Vivid Vision: Create a shorter (around 3 pages) Vivid Vision for your family.

CONCLUSION: VISION BECOMES REALITY

  • One Life, One Shot: We have one life and one opportunity to build a great company and achieve success in our careers.
  • The Vivid Vision as a Map: The Vivid Vision is a map that guides you toward your desired destination.
  • The Vivid Vision as a Script: The Vivid Vision outlines the future, ensuring everyone understands their role in achieving it.
  • Provide the Means of Seeing: Your primary responsibility is to articulate your vision so clearly that everyone in the organization can see it and contribute to its realization.
  • Trust the Process: Embrace the Vivid Vision process and see where it takes you.

STEP-BY-STEP CHECKLIST FOR CREATING A VIVID VISION DOCUMENT

Phase 1: Preparation & Mindset

  • Understand the Purpose: Align, inspire, guide, attract.
  • Choose Timeframe: Typically three years.
  • Find Your Inspiration Zone: A place for creativity.
  • Gather Tools: Notebook, whiteboard, computer, etc.
  • Set the Mental Stage: Clear your mind, focus on the future.

Phase 2: Brainstorming (Mind Map)

  • Create a Mind Map: Central node = company in 3 years.
  • Branch Out: Key business areas (marketing, sales, operations, etc.).
  • Expand Branches: Details for each area: What, how, feel, what are people saying, metrics, atmosphere, informal codes.
  • Time Machine Exercise: Imagine walking through your company in 3 years. Engage all senses.
  • Don't Filter: Capture all ideas, no judgment.

Phase 3: Drafting the Document

  • Narrative Form: Translate Mind Map into a story, present tense.
  • Focus on "What," Not "How": Describe the end state.
  • Be Specific: Use concrete language and details.
  • Incorporate Emotion: Describe how it feels, engage senses.
  • Keep it Concise: Aim for under four pages.

Phase 4: Refining & Polishing

  • Get Feedback (Optional): Share with trusted advisors.
  • Revise & Edit: Improve clarity, flow, and impact.
  • Engage a Writer (Optional): For professional polish.
  • Design & Format: Make it visually appealing.

Phase 5: Implementation & Rollout

  • Internal Rollout: Share with team, follow process (hard copy, read aloud, etc.).
  • External Rollout: Share with stakeholders after one quarter.
  • Regular Review: Revisit quarterly, highlight progress, plan next steps.
  • Live the Vision: Embody the values, make aligned decisions.

Key Reminders:

  • CEO Ownership: You are the primary driver.
  • Iterative Process: It takes time and refinement.
  • Focus on Inspiration: Motivate and align your team.
  • Make it a Living Document: Revisit and reinforce regularly.
